About

What is Callisto?

A place to share your work. Round-trip those notebooks with JupyterHub, show results, and keep the data.

Which data do you store?

We store basic account information (user name, e-mail). We store the documents you shared, comments, and likes.

Remember that until the document is published, it is only visible to you.

You can always delete a document or the whole Callisto account from the Dashboard. If you delete the Callisto account, all associated information (documents, comments, likes) will be purged as well.

FAQ

How do I share a document?

Sharing a document is a two step process:

  1. Start by uploading a document (either from file, or from the JupyterHub),
  2. Next, use the Publish button from your Dashboard.
When you publish a document you will have an opportunity to provide extra information. Other people will be able to discover it in the list of documents, or in Search.

If you change your mind you can always update the document or its information, or delete it altogether.

How do I run one of the notebooks?

Getting a notebook copied into your own JupyterHub session is a simple click. When you see a notebook on the Callisto that you'd like to run just press the open as notebook or Open as JupyterHub buttons which are on list of documents. If your notebook directory already contains a notebook of the same name it will make sure to append the current date/time to the newly imported notebook, as to not overwrite the existing one.

Can I edit/delete documents I've shared?

Yes, you can. Head to your Dashboard (My Documents in the menu bar above). There you can delete a document or select edit description to change the tags/sources/description of a document you have published.

Note that the edit description does not give a way to edit the document itself, but just the associated metadata. To also replace the code document itself you will have to replace it as outlined above in How do I share a document?

Why isn't my document on the Explore page? / What are the Other Documents I see when looking at some documents?

We are grouping documents by their name. So if you are submitting a document called mynotebook.ipynb, but there is already another document of the same name we assume that your document is a (potentially edited) copy of this document. To keep things a bit more organized we are grouping these together and only list the oldest document with that name on the Explore page.

If your document isn't an edited copy of an earlier document and you want to avoid being grouped with an existing document you should rename your document prior to publishing it.
